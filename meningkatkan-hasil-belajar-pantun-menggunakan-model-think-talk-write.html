4.

The structure of a personal letter typically includes the following elements:

Sender's Address: Start the letter by writing your own address at the top right corner of the page. Include your name, street address, city, state, and postal code.

Date: Below the sender's address, write the date when the letter is being written. It's usually placed a few lines below the sender's address, aligned with the left margin.

Recipient's Address: Leave a few lines after the date and write the recipient's address. Include the recipient's name, street address, city, state, and postal code. Align it with the left margin.

Salutation: Begin the letter with a salutation or greeting. Use a formal greeting such as "Dear" followed by the recipient's name. If you have a close relationship with the recipient, you may use a more informal greeting.

Introduction: In the first paragraph, introduce yourself and state the purpose of the letter. Mention any relevant information or context to set the tone and provide background if necessary.

Body: The body of the letter contains the main content. It can be divided into multiple paragraphs to address different points or topics. Use clear and concise language to express your thoughts, ideas, or feelings. Provide relevant details, examples, or anecdotes to support your message.

Conclusion: In the closing paragraph, summarize your main points or restate the purpose of the letter. Express any final thoughts, well wishes, or gratitude you may have.

Closing and Signature: Use a closing phrase such as "Sincerely," "Best regards," or "Yours truly" followed by a comma. Leave a few lines of space for your signature and then type your full name beneath it.

Postscript (Optional): You may include a postscript (P.S.) after your signature to add a brief additional note or mention something you forgot to include in the body of the letter.

Enclosures (If applicable): If you are including any additional documents or items along with the letter, mention them at the bottom of the letter by writing "Enclosures" or "Attachments" followed by a list of the enclosed items

PENJELASAN

DEFINITION:

Simple lresent tense adalah, kalimat yang menyatakan fakta/kebenaran umum, kejadian berulang/kebiasaan

contoh:

FACT: A sun rises from east

HABITUAL: The earth is always rotating every 24 hours

In Simple Present tense, the v-1 is always

ended with s/es for 3rd person singular(He, She, It, Name of person) and plural object

ex: 1 box 2 box(es)

1 match 3 match(es)

AKHIRAN KATA KERJA/OBJEK MENGGUNAKAN (ES) ADALAH

O

ex: go→ goes

do→does

CH

ex: match→matches

watch→watches

SH

ex: wash→washes

SS

ex: pass→passes

X

ex: fix→fixes

Y(i+es/s)

ex: study→studies

play→plays

Lalu rumus simple present tense

(+) subject+v1 + s/es+object + complement

ex:Rani reads a book every night

kalau formnya positif, dan subjectnya merupakan 3rd person singular (orang tunggal ketiga) kata kerja dalam bentuk form positif

wajib menggunakan akhiran s/es

(-) subject+do/does not/doesn't(biar lebih singkat)+object+complement

ex:Rani doesn't read a book every night

kalau formnya negatif, v1 itu balik ke awal tanpa menggunakan s/es

(?) does/do+subject+v1+object+complement

ex:does Rani read a book every night

sama dengan form negatif, tidak menggunakan s/es pada akhiran v1

PEMBAHASAN

Dalam simple present tense, kalimat positifnya menggunakan V1 (s/es). Kita gunakan :

Hanya Verb-1 ketika subjectnya I/you/they/we/plural subject. Sedangkan untuk the 3rd singular person (orang ketiga tunggal) seperti she/he/it/nama orang atau benda tunggal, kita menggunakan verb-1 ditambah s/es/ies.

Lantas, kata kerja yang bagaimana yang bisa kita tambahkan -s/es/ies? Berikut ini adalah penjelasanya :

Tambahkan -es

↪ Jika kata kerjanya berakhiran huruf -s, o, x, ch atau sh.

Contoh :

Discuss => DiscussesGo => GoesMix => MixesResearch => ResearchesWash => Washes

Tambahkan -ies

↪ Jika kata kerjanya berakhiran huruf -y tapi sebelum huruf -y ada huruf mati. Maka huruf -y dihilangkan.

Contoh :

Classify => ClassifiesSupply => SuppliesDry => Dries

Tambahkan -s

↪ Jika kata kerjanya tidak memiliki ciri-ciri akhiran seperti yang telah disebutkan di atas.

Contoh :

Eat => EatsDrink => DrinksEnjoy => EnjoysLearn more about :

Simple Present

34. Think of the three ways to conserve or recycle water at home. Talk about it or explain


Jawaban:

1. Check your toilet for leaks.

Put a few drops of food coloring in your toilet tank. If, without flushing, the coloring begins to appear in the bowl., you have a leak that may be wasting more than 100 gallons of water a day.

2. Stop using your toilet as an ashtray or wastebasket

Every cigarette butt or tissue you flush away also flushes away five to seven gallons of water.

3. Put a plastic bottle in your toilet tank

Put an inch or two of sand or pebbles in the bottom of a one liter bottle to weigh it down. Fill the rest of the bottle with water and put it in your toilet tank, safely away from the operating mechanism. In an average home, the bottle may save five gallons or more of water every day without harming the efficiency of the toilet. If your tank is big enough, you may even be able to put in two bottles.

4. Take shorter showers

A typical shower uses five to ten gallons of water a minute. Limit your showers to the time it takes to soap up, wash down and rise off.

5. Install water-saving shower heads or flow restrictors

Your hardware or plumbing supply store stocks inexpensive shower heads or flow restrictors that will cut your shower flow to about three gallons a minute instead of five to ten. They are easy to install, and your showers will still be cleansing and refreshing.

6. Take baths

A partially filled tub uses less water than all but the shortest showers.

7. Turn off the water while brushing your teeth

Before brushing, wet your brush and fill a glass for rinsing your mouth.

8. Turn off the water while shaving

Fill the bottom of the sink with a few inches of warm water in which to rinse your razor.

9. Check faucets and pipes for leaks

Even a small drip can waste 50 or more gallons of water a day.

10. Use your automatic dishwasher for full loads only

Every time you run your dishwasher, you use about 25 gallons of water.

11. Use your automatic washing machine only for full loads only

Your automatic washer uses 30 to 35 gallons per cycle.

12. Don't let the faucet run while you clean vegetables

Rinse your vegetables instead in a bowl or sink full of clean water.

13. Keep a bottle of drinking water in the refrigerator

This puts a stop to the wasteful practice of running tap water to cool it for drinking.

14. If you wash dishes by hand, don't leave the water running for rinsing

If you have two sinks, fill one with rinse water. If you have only one sink, first gather all your washed dishes in a dish rack, then rinse them quickly with a spray device or a pan of water.

15. Check faucets and pipes for leaks

Leaks waste water 24 hours a day, seven days a week. An inexpensive washer is usually enough to stop them.

16. Water your lawn only when it needs it

Watering on a regular schedule doesn't allow for cool spells or rainfall which reduce the need for watering. Step on some grass. If it springs back up when you move your foot, it doesn't need water
